Retire an offering — stop selling it. Operator-only, /confirm-gated.

    Sets ``lifecycle_status='retired'`` and ``is_sellable=false``, and stamps
    ``valid_to=now()`` on any still-open window. There is no hard delete and no
    one-call undo: bringing a retired plan back means a fresh
    ``catalog.window_offering`` and a lifecycle change.

    ELICIT BEFORE YOU CALL. Retire the offering the operator NAMED. If they said
    "retire the old lite plan" and two candidates match, list them with
    ``catalog.list_offerings`` and ask which — never pick the closest match.

    Required:
        offering_id: The offering to retire.

    Before proposing, read ``catalog.get_offering(offering_id)`` and tell the
    operator what they are retiring (id, name, current price). Existing
    subscriptions are NOT terminated — they keep renewing on their order-time
    price snapshot; retiring only stops NEW orders. Say that in your proposal:
    operators reasonably expect "retire" to mean "cancel the customers too", and
    it does not.

    Returns:
        Updated TMF620 offering dict.

    Raises:
        MISSING_REQUIRED_FIELDS: no offering_id supplied.
        PolicyViolationFromServer:
            - ``catalog.offering.not_found``: unknown id, or already retired.
